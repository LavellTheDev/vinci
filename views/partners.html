Air Craft Maintenance

Client Platform

Air Force Instructors struggle to ensure their airmen are prepared for the complexity of aircraft maintenance; units often don't have access to instructional aircraft or have limited time to utilize physical training simulators. Virtual Reality can address these shortages while ensuring airmen have an engaging learning resource to prepare for their mission.

VINCI was awarded a contract by the Air Force to develop VR environments and modify CODEX for various USAF aircraft.

Wind Turbines

Client Platform

VINCI is working with Siemens Gamesa Renewable Energy to prototype training solutions for safety equipment in their offshore wind turbine division. Currently, training is focused on helping workers identify errors on safety/fall harnesses. SGRE is challenged with visually showing defects on harnesses to their workforce, but must juggle the costs of doing so as demonstrating defects could damage expensive harnesses. VR can simulate an endless combination of possible defects at no cost, providing an immense amount of training opportunities for their workforce.

SafetyDNA VR

Client Platform

Teaching personal safety in the automobile manufacturing industry is a difficult task, but it is a deparately needed one. Every year, thousands of Americans are injured on manufacturing floors due to largely preventable causes.

Working with Select Int., VINCI helped prototype a VR version of their SAFETYDNA workplace safety training; workers can now get the experience of being injured due to unsafe actions in VR without the actual physical consequences. By doing this, the principles of SafetyDNA can be deeply embedded into workers’ minds, making them remember safety traits better on real-world lines.
